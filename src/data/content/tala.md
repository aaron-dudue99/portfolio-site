## Overview

Most wellness and fitness apps are loud. They gamify everything, push notifications constantly, and are built around streaks, badges, and upsells designed to maximise engagement rather than outcomes.

TALA is the opposite. It's a private, clinical-grade pelvic floor training app for men, built around the idea that a wellness tool should feel like a calm, trustworthy utility — not a game. There's no chasing engagement metrics here, no social feed, no noise. Just a structured training program, honest progress tracking, and a space that respects the user's privacy and attention.

---

## The Problem Up Close

Pelvic floor health is a topic most men never get structured guidance on, despite its role in core strength, continence, and sexual health. What little content exists online is scattered, inconsistently sourced, and often wrapped in the same hyperactive, ad-driven design language as every other fitness app.

TALA set out to fix this by combining two things that rarely appear together: a genuinely clinical approach to training (grounded in published guidance) and a calm, editorial-style interface that feels more like a premium personal journal than a tracker.

---

## How It Works

At its core, TALA guides a user through a **12-week structured training program**, built around three phases of pelvic floor conditioning: warming up circulation, actively relaxing the pelvic floor, and then building endurance through timed squeeze-and-hold intervals.

**The Trainer** is the heart of the app — an animated ring timer walks the user through each rep in real time, cueing them through preparing, squeezing, and releasing. Sessions are broken into sets, with a short celebration and rest prompt between them, and the app keeps track of exactly where the user is in today's session and this week's program.

**The Dashboard** gives a quick daily snapshot: a weekly consistency calendar, today's target hold and rest times, a simple pre-session warm-up checklist, and a rolling chart of stamina trends over time.

**Progress** turns individual sessions into a longer story — total time invested, current streak, how far through the 12-week program the user is, and a history of recent sessions.

**Wellness** is a small, curated article library covering pelvic floor health, sexual health, mental health, and general physical health — each article reviewed and sourced, not just generated filler content.

For users who want more control, a **Power User Mode** unlocks a custom plan builder, letting them override the clinical defaults with their own squeeze, rest, and rep targets — while keeping a safe minimum rest ratio enforced behind the scenes.

## Design Philosophy

A few principles guided every decision on this project:

**Calm over gamified.** No aggressive streak-shaming, no push notifications packed with exclamation marks, no leaderboard. Progress is shown honestly, without manufactured urgency.

**Privacy by default.** This is a sensitive, personal health topic. Every user's data is isolated at the database level, and even the daily reminder notification is worded discreetly — it never references pelvic exercises or sexual health on a lock screen.

**Editorial, not clinical-cold.** The interface leans into a warm, espresso-and-alabaster colour palette and spacious layout more reminiscent of a premium print magazine than a typical dark-mode fitness tracker — clinical rigor underneath, but a soft, human interface on top.

**Structure with an escape hatch.** The default experience is a guided, clinically-grounded program — but for users who know what they want, Power User Mode gets out of the way and lets them configure their own plan.

---

## Deliberate Trade-Offs

**No social or community features.** This is a deliberately private, single-user experience. Given the sensitivity of the subject matter, adding any social layer felt like the wrong direction entirely.

**Notifications kept minimal and discreet.** Rather than building an aggressive reminder system, TALA sends a single, softly-worded daily nudge — and even that requires an explicit opt-in.

**No wearable integration (yet).** The app currently relies on manual, guided sessions rather than pulling in data from external devices, keeping the initial scope focused on getting the core training experience right.

## Current State & What's Next

TALA has reached its first complete version: full authentication and onboarding, the guided 12-week clinical program, the animated trainer experience, progress tracking, a wellness article library, and a custom plan builder for advanced users.

It's currently going through the process of being prepared for release on the Google Play Store, including the closed testing phase Google requires before a wellness app can go fully public.

## What This Project Represents

TALA is a personal, independently-built project — a chance to design and ship an entire product end to end, from clinical research and content sourcing through to interface design and a real app store release. It reflects a belief that sensitive, personal health topics deserve tools that are quiet, respectful, and genuinely useful — not just another app competing for attention.
